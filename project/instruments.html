Instruments and Their Families

Musical instruments are divided into families based on how they produce sound. Here are the main families:

Strings

String instruments produce sound by vibrating strings. They can be plucked, bowed, or struck. Common string instruments include:

  • Violin
  • Viola
  • Cello
  • Double Bass
  • Harp
  • Guitar

Woodwinds

Woodwind instruments produce sound by splitting an airstream on a sharp edge, such as a reed or a mouthpiece. Common woodwind instruments include:

  • Flute
  • Clarinet
  • Oboe
  • Bassoon
  • Saxophone

Brass

Brass instruments produce sound by buzzing the lips into a mouthpiece. Common brass instruments include:

  • Trumpet
  • Trombone
  • French Horn
  • Tuba
  • Euphonium

Percussion

Percussion instruments produce sound by being struck, shaken, or scraped. They can be pitched or unpitched. Common percussion instruments include:

  • Snare Drum
  • Bass Drum
  • Tambourine
  • Marimba
  • Timpani
  • Cymbals

Keyboard

Keyboard instruments produce sound by pressing keys that cause hammers to strike strings, air to be blown through pipes, or electronic sounds to be produced. Common keyboard instruments include:

  • Piano
  • Organ
  • Harpsichord
  • Electronic Keyboard
